🔬 What is LH & why does it matter?
Luteinizing hormone (LH) is released by the pituitary gland and triggers ovulation — the release of an egg. LH surges 24–48 hours before ovulation, making it the most reliable predictor of the fertile window.

A positive LH test (when the test line is as dark or darker than the control line) means ovulation is likely within the next day or two. This is the most fertile time in the cycle.
Common myths, busted
❌ "Ovulation always happens on day 14"
This is only true for a textbook 28-day cycle. In reality, ovulation can happen anywhere from day 11 to day 21 depending on cycle length and stress levels. LH testing is far more reliable than counting days.
❌ "You can't get pregnant outside the fertile window"
Sperm can survive inside the body for up to 5 days, meaning intercourse several days before ovulation can still result in conception. The fertile window is generally days 10–17, not just the day of ovulation.
❌ "PMS is just emotional — it's not physical"
PMS has documented physical and hormonal causes. In the luteal phase, progesterone rises sharply and then drops before menstruation, which directly affects serotonin levels, causing mood shifts, bloating, and fatigue.
